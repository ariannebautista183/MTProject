Explore Bacarra

A town with heritage.

Bacarra Bell Tower

he Bacarra Bell Tower is situated in the town of Bacarra, in the province of Ilocos Norte, Philippines. It stands as a prominent historical landmark in the town center, making it easily accessible for visitors who are exploring the Ilocos region.

Bacarra River

The Bacarra River originates from the Bislak RIver that flows from the spring water of the Cordilleras. The Bacarra River is the setting of the believed story about the origin of “Bacarra” as the townname. It was a fishing target of the nearby residents and non-poblacion residents.

Natba Beach and Delta Tourism Area

Bacarra’s Natba Beach has silvery sand and cool waters. It was one of the most splendid shores in the north. Natba Beach had continuously attracted both local people and foreigners with its scenic sunset and unpredictable delta and shoreline. The cleanest delta (puyupuyan) in the province accentuated by the historical Ruins of the Vijia watchtower.

Calioet Beach

Calioet Beach is located at Brgy. #31 Calioet Libong and like the Natba Beach it also serves as another tourist spot. Facilities that can be found include municipal picnic shed with shower room and barangay picnic sheds. Private cottages are also present within the area.

Bacarra Old Spanish Watchtower

One of the few Spanish watchtowers which dotted the coastlines from Ilocos Norte to La Union, to guard against any attack offshore. Also known as the Natba Watchtower, named after the barangay where it is situated, once a derelict (with one side split and has crumbled to the ground) with overgrown plants in and around the mound.
